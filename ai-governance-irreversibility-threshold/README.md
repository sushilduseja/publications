# Beyond Remediation: An Irreversibility Threshold for Preventive AI Governance

Existing AI governance frameworks assume most harms can be detected, attributed,
and remediated after the fact. This paper identifies three harm categories where
that assumption breaks down: epistemic contagion, reputational destruction, and
structural labor displacement; and proposes the Systemic Irreversibility Baseline
as an operational threshold that routes each harm class to a distinct governance
track.

The analysis covers four Euro-Atlantic regulatory instruments: the EU AI Act,
the NIST AI RMF, the UK AI White Paper, and the OECD incident-reporting
framework. Four technical design mandates operationalize the precautionary track.
